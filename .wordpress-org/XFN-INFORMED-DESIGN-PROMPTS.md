### Core Concept
XFN enables web authors to **represent human relationships through hyperlinks** by adding a simple `rel` attribute to HTML anchor tags.

Example: `<a href="https://example.com" rel="friend met colleague">`

### Design Philosophy
1. **Simplicity** - "As simple as possible, but no more"
2. **Decentralization** - Distributed like the web itself
3. **Positive relationships** - No negative terms
4. **Relationship-focused** - Connections between people, not attributes

### Visual Metaphors
- **Human connections** (social network)
- **Web links** (chains, hyperlinks)
- **Decentralized network** (nodes and connections)
- **Blogroll era** (early 2000s social web)
- **Seven relationship types**: Friendship, Physical, Professional, Geographical, Family, Romantic, Identity

### Era & Context
- Created during blogging boom (2003)
- SxSW conference origin
- Pre-dates Facebook, Twitter (pioneer of social metadata)
- Part of semantic web movement
